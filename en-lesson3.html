Grammar Focus

  • Noun: a person, place, thing, or idea (e.g., teacher, park).
  • Verb: an action word (e.g., run, jump, think).
  • Adjective: a describing word (e.g., happy, tall).
  • Adverb: tells more about the verb (e.g., quickly, softly).
  • Sentence: A group of words that express a complete thought (begins with capital, ends with punctuation).

Worked Examples

Example 1: Word: brave → Sentence: The brave firefighter climbed the ladder.

Example 2: Word: silent → Sentence: The library was very silent.

Example 3: Grammar: Identify the noun — "The cat chased the mouse." Nouns: cat, mouse.

Example 4: Grammar: Find the verb — "She runs every morning." Verb: runs.

Example 5: Grammar: Find the adjective — "The tall tree touched the sky." Adjective: tall.

Example 6: Grammar: Find the adverb — "He sang loudly." Adverb: loudly.

Example 7: Correct sentence: "We are happy today." (Capital + punctuation)

Example 8: Wrong vs Right → Wrong: "she play soccer" | Right: "She plays soccer."

Mini Tips

  2. Check if your sentence has a noun + verb.
  3. Adjectives describe; adverbs tell how.
