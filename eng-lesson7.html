Week 4 — English Lesson Notes

Focus: Spelling • Grammar • Reading • Vocabulary • Comprehension

Lesson Summary

This week focused on reinforcing spelling, comprehension, and grammar through practical exercises and contextual reading. Nathan practiced sentence formation, short paragraph reading, and discussed short stories to enhance inferential understanding and vocabulary. In grammar, he applied parts of speech to form accurate and meaningful sentences, strengthening his communication and writing clarity. His confidence and enthusiasm for learning continue to grow impressively.

Key Vocabulary (10 Words)

  • Infer — to draw a conclusion from clues
  • Context — the surrounding words that help explain meaning
  • Fluency — reading smoothly and clearly
  • Accuracy — correctness in spelling or grammar
  • Confidence — belief in one’s ability
  • Communication — expressing ideas clearly
  • Sentence — a complete thought with subject and verb
  • Paragraph — a group of related sentences
  • Expression — showing thoughts or feelings through words
  • Enthusiasm — eager interest or excitement

Reading & Comprehension

Before You Read: Think of a time when you learned a new word from a story. What helped you understand it?

Short Story: The Lost Pencil

Nathan’s pencil was missing. He searched everywhere—his desk, his bag, even under the table. Finally, he remembered lending it to his friend earlier that morning. When he asked, his friend smiled and returned it, saying, “I was keeping it safe for you.” Nathan laughed, learning that sometimes, what we think is lost is simply forgotten.

Check Your Understanding

  1. Who lost the pencil?
  2. Where did Nathan search for it?
  3. Who had the pencil?
  4. What lesson did Nathan learn?
  5. What does the word “forgotten” mean in this story?
  6. Write one adjective that describes Nathan’s friend.
  7. What could Nathan do next time to avoid losing things?
  8. What kind of story is this (funny, serious, mystery)?
  9. Which sentence shows kindness in the story?
  10. Explain in one sentence what you think the story teaches us.

Grammar Practice — Parts of Speech

Identify and use parts of speech correctly in sentences.

Exercise: Fill in each blank with the correct word type.

  1. Nathan runs very __________. (adverb)
  2. The __________ pencil was blue. (adjective)
  3. He __________ his bag carefully. (verb)
  4. His friend gave __________ a smile. (pronoun)
  5. They searched __________ the room. (preposition)

Spelling & Writing Practice

Write five new sentences using at least one vocabulary word in each sentence.
