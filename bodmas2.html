Lesson: Order of Operations (BODMAS)

In mathematics, the order in which we carry out operations is very important. If we do not follow the correct order, we will get different answers. The rule we use is called BODMAS:

  • B — Brackets
  • O — Orders (powers, roots)
  • D — Division
  • M — Multiplication
  • A — Addition
  • S — Subtraction

Example 1

Evaluate: \( 8 + 2 \times 5 \)

Step 1: According to BODMAS, do multiplication before addition. \( 2 \times 5 = 10 \) Step 2: Add 8 + 10 = 18.

Example 2

Evaluate: \( (6 + 4) \times 3 \)

Step 1: Solve inside the brackets first. \( 6 + 4 = 10 \). Step 2: Multiply: \( 10 \times 3 = 30 \). Final Answer = 30.

Example 3

Evaluate: \( 100 \div (5 \times 2) \)

Step 1: Inside brackets: \( 5 \times 2 = 10 \). Step 2: Divide: \( 100 \div 10 = 10 \). Final Answer = 10.

Worked Examples

Example 1

Evaluate: \( 6 + 2 \times 3 \)

Step 1: Multiplication first → \( 2 \times 3 = 6 \).
Step 2: Add \( 6 + 6 = 12 \).
Final Answer = 12.

Example 2

Evaluate: \( (10 - 4) \times 2 \)

Step 1: Brackets first → \( 10 - 4 = 6 \).
Step 2: Multiply → \( 6 \times 2 = 12 \).
Final Answer = 12.

Example 3

Evaluate: \( 18 \div 3 + 2^2 \)

Step 1: Orders (powers) → \( 2^2 = 4 \).
Step 2: Division → \( 18 \div 3 = 6 \).
Step 3: Add → \( 6 + 4 = 10 \).
Final Answer = 10.

Example 4

Evaluate: \( (20 \div 5) + (3 \times 4) \)

Step 1: Inside brackets → \( 20 \div 5 = 4 \), \( 3 \times 4 = 12 \).
Step 2: Add → \( 4 + 12 = 16 \).
Final Answer = 16.

Example 5

Evaluate: \( 100 - (6 + 2) \times 8 \)

Step 1: Inside brackets → \( 6 + 2 = 8 \).
Step 2: Multiply → \( 8 \times 8 = 64 \).
Step 3: Subtract → \( 100 - 64 = 36 \).
Final Answer = 36.

Example 6

Evaluate: \( (12 - 2)^2 \div 5 \)

Step 1: Inside brackets → \( 12 - 2 = 10 \).
Step 2: Orders → \( 10^2 = 100 \).
Step 3: Division → \( 100 \div 5 = 20 \).
Final Answer = 20.

Example 7

Evaluate: \( 60 \div [2 \times (5 + 5)] \)

Step 1: Inner brackets → \( 5 + 5 = 10 \).
Step 2: Multiply inside square brackets → \( 2 \times 10 = 20 \).
Step 3: Divide → \( 60 \div 20 = 3 \).
Final Answer = 3.

Example 8

Evaluate: \( \{ [ (3 + 5) \times 2 ]^2 \div 8 \} - 7 \)

Step 1: Innermost brackets → \( 3 + 5 = 8 \).
Step 2: Multiply → \( 8 \times 2 = 16 \).
Step 3: Square → \( 16^2 = 256 \).
Step 4: Divide → \( 256 \div 8 = 32 \).
Step 5: Subtract → \( 32 - 7 = 25 \).
Final Answer = 25.
