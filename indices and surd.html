Topic: Surds and Indices

In mathematics, **indices** (powers) and **surds** (roots left in exact form) are essential tools. Understanding how to simplify and manipulate them is a key part of algebra.

Key Concepts

  • Index laws: \[ a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}, \quad \frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n}, \quad (a^m)^n = a^{mn} \]
  • Negative and fractional indices: \[ a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}, \quad a^{\frac{1}{n}} = \sqrt[n]{a} \]
  • Surds: irrational roots expressed in exact form, e.g. \(\sqrt{2}, \sqrt[3]{5}\).

Worked Examples

Example 1: Simplify \( 2^3 \times 2^5 \).

By index law: \( 2^{3+5} = 2^8 = 256 \).

Example 2: Simplify \( \frac{5^7}{5^3} \).

Subtract powers: \( 5^{7-3} = 5^4 = 625 \).

Example 3: Simplify \( (3^2)^4 \).

Multiply indices: \( 3^{2 \times 4} = 3^8 = 6561 \).

Example 4: Simplify \( 16^{\tfrac{1}{2}} \).

This is the square root: \( \sqrt{16} = 4 \).

Example 6: Simplify \( \sqrt{50} \).

Factorize 50 = 25 Ã— 2. So \( \sqrt{50} = \sqrt{25 \times 2} = \sqrt{25} \times \sqrt{2} = 5\sqrt{2} \).

Example 7: Rationalize denominator: \( \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \).

Multiply top and bottom by \(\sqrt{3}\): \( \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \).

Example 8: Simplify \( \frac{\sqrt{18}}{\sqrt{2}} \).

Combine under one root: \( \sqrt{\tfrac{18}{2}} = \sqrt{9} = 3 \).
