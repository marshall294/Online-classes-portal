1. What is a Ratio?

A ratio compares two or more quantities showing how many times one value contains another. Ratios are written in three common ways:

  • a to b (words)
  • a:b (colon form)
  • a/b (fraction form)

Example: If there are 8 blue pens and 4 red pens, the ratio of blue to red = 8:4, which can be read as "8 to 4".

How to simplify a ratio (step-by-step)

  1. Find the Highest Common Factor (HCF) of the parts (also called GCF).
  2. Divide each part by the HCF.
  3. Write the simplified ratio in the form a:b.

Worked Example — Simplify 12:18

Step 1: Find HCF of 12 and 18. HCF = 6.

Step 2: Divide both numbers by 6: 12 ÷ 6 = 2, 18 ÷ 6 = 3.

Answer: 12:18 = 2:3.

2. Equivalent Ratios and the Unitary Method

Equivalent ratios are made by multiplying or dividing both parts by the same number.

If the ratio is 3:5, multiply both parts by 2 to get 6:10 — both show the same relationship.

Unitary method (finding value of 1 part)

  1. Add the ratio parts to find total parts.
  2. Divide the total quantity by the total parts to get the value of one part.
  3. Multiply that value by each part to find each share.

Worked Example — Divide $60 in ratio 2:3

Step 1: Total parts = 2 + 3 = 5.

Step 2: Value of 1 part = 60 ÷ 5 = 12.

Step 3: First share = 2 × 12 = $24. Second share = 3 × 12 = $36.

Answer: $24 and $36.

3. Direct Proportion

Two quantities are in direct proportion when they increase or decrease together at the same rate. If y is directly proportional to x, we write y ∝ x or y = kx, where k is the constant of proportionality.

How to solve direct proportion problems (methods)

  1. Unitary method: Find value per 1 unit, then scale up.
  2. Use ratio: Set up a : b = x : y and solve by multiplying across.
  3. Algebra: Use y1/x1 = y2/x2 (because y/x = k) or find k from y = kx.

Example — If 7 oranges cost $14, what do 12 oranges cost?

Method 1 (Unitary):

  1. Cost of 1 orange = 14 ÷ 7 = $2.
  2. Cost of 12 oranges = 12 × 2 = $24.

Answer: $24.

Method 2 (Proportion): 7 : 14 = 12 : x → 7/14 = 12/x → 1/2 = 12/x → x = 24.

4. Inverse Proportion

Two quantities are in inverse proportion when one quantity increases while the other decreases so that the product remains constant. If y is inversely proportional to x, we write y ∝ 1/x or y = k/x.

That means x × y = k (a constant). Use this to solve word problems: find the product for the known pair, then use it to find the unknown.

Example — 6 workers take 10 days. How many days will 15 workers take?

Step 1: Compute the product (workers × days) = 6 × 10 = 60. This product stays the same.

Step 2: Let unknown days = d. Then 15 × d = 60 ⇒ d = 60 ÷ 15 = 4.

Answer: 4 days.

5. Graphs & Visual Ideas

Direct proportion graphs are straight lines through the origin (0,0). The slope is the constant k. Inverse proportion graphs are curved (a hyperbola) and never touch the axes for positive values.

6. A Simple Strategy for Answering Word Problems

  1. Read the problem carefully and underline the quantities given and what is asked.
  2. Decide whether it is direct or inverse proportion (or just ratio/unitary).
  3. Choose a method: unitary, ratio, algebra (y=kx) or product method for inverse.
  4. Set up equations and solve step-by-step, showing your working.
  5. Check your answer by plugging it back to see if it makes sense (units, size, common sense).

7. More Worked Examples (step-by-step)

Example: It takes 12 men 8 days to build a wall. How long for 6 men?

Type: Inverse proportion because more men ⇒ fewer days.

Step 1: product = men × days = 12 × 8 = 96.

Step 2: Let unknown days = d for 6 men → 6 × d = 96 ⇒ d = 96 ÷ 6 = 16 days.

Answer: 16 days.

Example: If 4 pencils cost $3, how much will 10 cost?

Type: Direct proportion (more pencils ⇒ more cost).

Step 1: cost per pencil = 3 ÷ 4 = $0.75.

Step 2: cost for 10 pencils = 10 × 0.75 = $7.50.

Answer: $7.50.

8. Assessment — 15 Questions

  1. Simplify the ratio 15:25.
  2. Divide $60 in the ratio 2:3.
  3. If 7 oranges cost $14, what is the cost of 12 oranges?
  4. If 8 men take 15 days to finish a job, how long will 12 men take?
  5. Express 45 minutes as a ratio of 2 hours.
  6. If 4 pencils cost $3, how much will 10 pencils cost?
  7. If 10 workers can dig a trench in 12 days, how many workers are needed to finish in 6 days?
  8. Simplify 120:150.
  9. A recipe calls for 2 cups of sugar for 5 cakes. How many cups are needed for 20 cakes?
  10. If 9 books cost $27, how much do 5 books cost?
  11. If 15 men build a wall in 18 days, how many days will 9 men take?
  12. Simplify the ratio 36:48.
  13. If a car travels 180 km in 3 hours, how far will it travel in 7 hours at the same speed?
  14. It takes 20 workers 24 days to complete a project. How long would 30 workers take?
  15. Divide $90 between A and B in the ratio 1:2.

Solutions (step-by-step)

  1. Question 1 — Simplify 15:25

    Find HCF of 15 and 25 → HCF = 5. Divide both terms by 5: 15 ÷ 5 = 3 and 25 ÷ 5 = 5. So 3:5.

  2. Question 2 — Divide $60 in ratio 2:3

    Total parts = 2 + 3 = 5. Value of one part = 60 ÷ 5 = 12. First share = 2 × 12 = $24. Second share = 3 × 12 = $36.

  3. Question 3 — 7 oranges cost $14; cost of 12 oranges?

    Cost per orange = 14 ÷ 7 = $2. Multiply by 12 → 12 × 2 = $24.

  4. Question 4 — 8 men take 15 days; how long for 12 men?

    Type: inverse proportion (more men → fewer days). Product = 8 × 15 = 120. For 12 men: 12 × d = 120 ⇒ d = 120 ÷ 12 = 10 days.

  5. Question 5 — 45 minutes as ratio of 2 hours

    Convert 2 hours to minutes: 2 × 60 = 120 minutes. Ratio = 45 : 120. Simplify: divide both by 15 → 45 ÷ 15 = 3, 120 ÷ 15 = 8 ⇒ 3:8.

  6. Question 6 — 4 pencils cost $3; 10 pencils cost?

    Cost per pencil = 3 ÷ 4 = $0.75. For 10 pencils: 10 × 0.75 = $7.50.

  7. Question 7 — 10 workers → 12 days. How many workers for 6 days?

    Inverse proportion: workers × days = constant. 10 × 12 = 120. Let workers = w: w × 6 = 120 ⇒ w = 120 ÷ 6 = 20 workers.

  8. Question 8 — Simplify 120:150

    Find HCF. HCF of 120 and 150 = 30. Divide: 120 ÷ 30 = 4, 150 ÷ 30 = 5 ⇒ 4:5.

  9. Question 9 — 2 cups sugar for 5 cakes. For 20 cakes?

    Find scale factor: 20 ÷ 5 = 4. Multiply sugar: 2 × 4 = 8 cups.

  10. Question 10 — 9 books cost $27; cost of 5 books?

    Price per book = 27 ÷ 9 = $3. So 5 books = 5 × 3 = $15.

  11. Question 11 — 15 men build in 18 days; how many days for 9 men?

    Inverse proportion. Constant product = 15 × 18 = 270. For 9 men: 9 × d = 270 ⇒ d = 270 ÷ 9 = 30 days.

  12. Question 12 — Simplify 36:48

    HCF of 36 and 48 = 12. Divide: 36 ÷ 12 = 3, 48 ÷ 12 = 4 ⇒ 3:4.

  13. Question 13 — 180 km in 3 hours — distance in 7 hours?

    Speed = 180 ÷ 3 = 60 km/h. Distance in 7 h = 7 × 60 = 420 km.

  14. Question 14 — 20 workers × 24 days. How long for 30 workers?

    Inverse proportion. Constant = 20 × 24 = 480. For 30 workers: 30 × d = 480 ⇒ d = 480 ÷ 30 = 16 days.

  15. Question 15 — Divide $90 in ratio 1:2

    Total parts = 1 + 2 = 3. Value per part = 90 ÷ 3 = 30. A gets 1 × 30 = $30. B gets 2 × 30 = $60.

Check your method: For direct proportion questions, you should get the same answer using unitary method, ratios, or algebra (y/x constant). For inverse proportion, always check the product (x × y) remains constant.
