Grade 5 English – Reading Skills Lesson 6

Comprehending Informational Texts

Lesson Objectives

  • Improve reading fluency and vocabulary through a short passage.
  • Identify main ideas and supporting details.
  • Develop inferencing and comprehension skills.
  • Express personal thoughts clearly in writing.

Before You Read

Look at the title: (The Mystery of the Missing Lunch) and imagine what might happen in the story. Type your predictions below.

Reading Passage: The Mystery of the Missing Lunch

Read carefully and think about what the story teaches.

At Maple Grove Primary School, something strange was happening. Every day at noon, one lunchbox disappeared from the classroom shelf. The students began to whisper and guess who might be taking it. Mrs. Luna, their teacher, decided to turn the problem into a mystery project.

Each group of students wrote down clues and kept an eye on the lunch area. On Friday, Mia noticed crumbs near the window and small muddy paw prints on the floor. The class waited quietly during lunch and soon saw a small brown puppy squeeze through the open window! Everyone laughed as the puppy wagged its tail and tried to lick the peanut-butter jar.

Mrs. Luna explained that the puppy belonged to the gardener and had been exploring the classrooms looking for snacks. The students helped clean the area and made a tiny doghouse for their new furry friend, whom they named “Scooter.” From that day, no lunch ever went missing again.

Key Vocabulary (10 Words)

  • Whisper: to speak very softly.
  • Clues: pieces of information that help solve a mystery.
  • Exploring: going somewhere to find new things.
  • Observe: to watch carefully.
  • Suspicious: feeling that something is wrong or someone might be guilty.
  • Evidence: information that proves something true.
  • Discover: to find out or learn something new.
  • Investigate: to study or look into a problem carefully.
  • Curious: eager to know or learn more.
  • Responsible: being the cause of something or taking care of duties.

Check Your Understanding

Answer each question in your own words. Think carefully and type complete sentences.

Reflect and Extend

Think about a time when you solved a mystery or discovered something unexpected. Write a short paragraph about what happened and what you learned.
