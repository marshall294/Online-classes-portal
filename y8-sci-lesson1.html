What is Respiration?

Respiration is a chemical process that takes place in the cells of all living organisms. It releases energy from food (mainly glucose) to power life processes such as movement, growth, repair, and keeping warm.

⚡ Without respiration, cells would not be able to survive.

[Diagram: Respiration as energy release → Movement, Growth, Repair, Warmth]
Example: When you run, your leg muscles contract repeatedly. This requires large amounts of energy, released by respiration in muscle cells.

Quick Check: Where does respiration occur in living organisms?

Aerobic Respiration

Aerobic respiration uses oxygen. It is the most efficient form of respiration and releases a large amount of energy.

Word Equation:
Glucose + Oxygen → Carbon dioxide + Water + Energy

Balanced Symbol Equation:
C6H12O6 + 6O2 → 6CO2 + 6H2O + Energy

[Diagram: Glucose + Oxygen entering mitochondria → Energy + CO₂ + H₂O]

Quick Check: Which organelle in the cell carries out aerobic respiration?

Anaerobic Respiration

Anaerobic respiration takes place when oxygen is not available. It is less efficient and releases less energy per glucose molecule. In animals, it produces lactic acid, which can cause muscle fatigue.

Equation: Glucose → Lactic acid + Energy

Sprinters often rely on anaerobic respiration during short bursts of high activity because oxygen supply cannot keep up with demand.

Quick Check: What is the main waste product of anaerobic respiration in animals?

Breathing vs Respiration

⚠️ Breathing is a mechanical process of inhaling oxygen and exhaling carbon dioxide using the lungs. ✅ Respiration is a chemical process that happens in cells to release energy.

Quick Check: Which of the two is a cellular chemical process?

Why Do We Need Energy?

  • Muscle contraction (movement)
  • Maintaining body temperature (warmth)
  • Active transport of substances across membranes
  • Cell division and repair
  • Protein synthesis
