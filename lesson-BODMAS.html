Order of Operations (BODMAS)

In mathematics, when more than one operation appears in a calculation, we must follow the correct order of operations. To help remember the order, we use the rule called BODMAS.

  • B â†’ Brackets
  • O â†’ Orders (Powers, Indices, Roots)
  • D â†’ Division
  • M â†’ Multiplication
  • A â†’ Addition
  • S â†’ Subtraction

Step-by-Step Explanation

When solving any expression:

  1. Solve inside Brackets first.
  2. Then calculate Orders (powers, indices, roots).
  3. Next, do Division and Multiplication (from left to right).
  4. Finally, do Addition and Subtraction (from left to right).

Worked Examples

Example 1: \( 3 + 5 \times 2 \)

Step 1: Multiplication first â†’ \( 5 \times 2 = 10 \)

Step 2: Add â†’ \( 3 + 10 = 13 \)

Answer: 13

Example 2: \( (6 + 4) \times 3 \)

Step 1: Brackets first â†’ \( 6 + 4 = 10 \)

Step 2: Multiply â†’ \( 10 \times 3 = 30 \)

Answer: 30

Example 3: \( 20 \div (2^2 + 2) \)

Step 1: Brackets â†’ \( 2^2 + 2 = 4 + 2 = 6 \)

Step 2: Divide â†’ \( 20 \div 6 = 3.\overline{3} \)

Answer: \( 3.\overline{3} \)

Example 4: \( 12 - 2 \times (3 + 1)^2 \)

Step 1: Brackets â†’ \( 3 + 1 = 4 \)

Step 2: Orders â†’ \( 4^2 = 16 \)

Step 3: Multiplication â†’ \( 2 \times 16 = 32 \)

Step 4: Subtraction â†’ \( 12 - 32 = -20 \)

Answer: -20
