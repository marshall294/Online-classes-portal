Spelling: Plurals & Rules

Easy rules + exceptions

When we want to talk about more than one thing, we usually add something to the end of a word. These are called **plural nouns**. Let's learn the rules for spelling them correctly. 👇

📘 Rule A — Most nouns: Just add -s to make them plural.

Example: book → books

  • dog → dogs
  • car → cars
  • tree → trees
  • apple → apples
  • pencil → pencils
  • hat → hats
📕 Rule B — Words ending in s, ss, sh, ch, x, or z: Add -es.

These words need an extra "e" to sound right when you say them.

Example: box → boxes

  • bus → buses
  • glass → glasses
  • brush → brushes
  • match → matches
  • fox → foxes
  • quiz → quizzes
  • church → churches
📗 Rule C — Words ending in a consonant + y: Change the y to i and add -es.

If the word ends in a vowel + y (like "toy"), just add -s.

Example: city → cities

  • puppy → puppies
  • party → parties
  • story → stories
  • berry → berries
  • baby → babies
  • lady → ladies
  • toy → toys (vowel + y)
  • key → keys (vowel + y)
  • monkey → monkeys (vowel + y)
📙 Rule D — Irregular plurals: These words don’t follow any rule. You just have to **memorize** them.

Example: child → children

  • mouse → mice
  • man → men
  • woman → women
  • foot → feet
  • tooth → teeth
  • goose → geese
  • person → people
  • sheep → sheep (same in singular and plural)
  • deer → deer (same in singular and plural)

💡 Teacher Tip
Say the word aloud. If the ending sounds like /s/ or /z/, add -s. If it sounds like /iz/ (like “buzzes” or “wishes”), add -es.
👂 Listening to how the word sounds can help you spell it right!

Reading Fluency — Punctuation

How punctuation affects reading

Punctuation marks are not just for writing — they help us **read with the right voice, tone, and pause**. Learning how to read punctuation makes your reading **more fluent, expressive, and easy to understand**.

📍 Full stop (.) — Take a complete pause. It shows the end of a sentence.
📍 Comma (,) — Take a short pause. It separates ideas, lists, or adds extra information.
📍 Question mark (?) — Use a rising tone. It shows a question or curiosity.
📍 Exclamation mark (!) — Use a strong voice. It shows excitement, surprise, or warning.

🗣️ Reading aloud tips:
✔️ Look at the punctuation before reading.
✔️ Breathe at commas; pause longer at full stops.
✔️ Raise your voice slightly at question marks.
✔️ Use emotion or energy for exclamation marks!

Learn words deeply

A Word Map helps you remember a word by connecting meaning, synonyms, antonyms and an example sentence.

Practice this for 8–10 new words per week.
