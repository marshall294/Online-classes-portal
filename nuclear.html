1?? Development of the Atom

Overview: The atomic model evolved over time as scientists conducted experiments to better understand atomic structure.

  • John Dalton (1800): Proposed atoms as indivisible solid spheres; each element has its own kind of atom.
  • J.J. Thomson (1897): Discovered the electron using cathode rays; proposed the “plum pudding model”.
  • Ernest Rutherford (1911): Gold foil experiment showed atoms have a small, dense, positive nucleus — most of the atom is empty space.
  • Niels Bohr (1913): Proposed electrons orbit in fixed energy levels (shells).
  • James Chadwick (1932): Discovered the neutron, explaining differences between atomic mass and atomic number.
Example: In Rutherford’s experiment, most a-particles passed through the gold foil (atom mostly empty), while some deflected — showing a dense nucleus.

4?? Half-Life Calculations

Definition: The half-life of a radioactive isotope is the time for half of its nuclei to decay.

Formula: N = N0(½)^(t/t½)

  • N = remaining activity or nuclei
  • N0 = initial activity
  • t = time elapsed
  • t½ = half-life
Example: A sample has 800 Bq and a half-life of 10 s.
After 30 s: N = 800 × (½)³ = 100 Bq

Applications: Carbon dating, medical tracers, and nuclear waste management.

5?? Irradiation and Contamination

Irradiation: Exposure to radiation from an external source. Does not make objects radioactive.

Contamination: When radioactive materials land on or enter an object/person. The object becomes radioactive until decontaminated or decayed.
