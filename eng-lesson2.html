Key Definitions (child-friendly)

  • Main idea ‚Äî the most important thing the passage is about.
  • Detail ‚Äî a small fact or piece of information from the passage.
  • Inference ‚Äî a smart guess we make using clues from the text and our own thinking.
  • Sequence ‚Äî the order in which events happen.
  • Summary ‚Äî a short version of the story using the main idea and important details.

Explanation ‚Äî How to read like a detective

  1. Preview: Look at the title and pictures. Guess what it might be about.
  2. Read: Read slowly and notice important words.
  3. Question: Ask who, what, when, where, why, and how as you read.
  4. Visualize: Make a movie in your head of the events.
  5. Check: After reading, say the main idea in one sentence.

8 Worked Examples (step-by-step)

Example 1 ‚Äî Vocabulary

Passage line: "The tiny boat drifted slowly toward the shore." What does "drifted" mean? Answer: It moved slowly without being pushed.

Example 2 ‚Äî Main idea

Short text: "Anna fed the birds every morning because she loved watching them." Main idea: Anna loves feeding birds each morning.

Example 3 ‚Äî Detail

Question: "What time did Anna feed the birds?" Look for small facts in the sentence: "every morning" is the detail.

Example 4 ‚Äî Inference

Text: "Sam wore a coat and carried an umbrella." Inference: It might be cold or rainy.

Example 5 ‚Äî Sequence

Text: "First she watered the plants, then she swept the porch, and finally she sat down." Order: water ‚Üí sweep ‚Üí sit.

Example 6 ‚Äî Summary

Short: "Tom helped the lost puppy find home." Summary: Tom helped a puppy return home.

Example 7 ‚Äî Author's purpose

Sentence: "Please recycle your bottles." Purpose: to persuade or give instruction.

Example 8 ‚Äî Cause & Effect

Text: "Because it rained, the game was canceled." Cause: it rained. Effect: game canceled.
