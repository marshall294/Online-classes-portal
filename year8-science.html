Year 8 Science — Photosynthesis

Hello Student! Welcome to Science Class.

Introduction

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants make their own food using light energy, water, and carbon dioxide. It takes place in the chloroplasts of plant cells, which contain the green pigment chlorophyll.

The word equation for photosynthesis is:

Carbon Dioxide + Water → Glucose + Oxygen

The balanced chemical equation is:

\( 6CO_2 + 6H_2O \xrightarrow{\text{light, chlorophyll}} C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2 \)

This process is vital because it produces oxygen for respiration and glucose for energy storage.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Write the word equation for photosynthesis.

Solution: Carbon Dioxide + Water → Glucose + Oxygen

Example 2: Balance the equation:

Solution: \( 6CO_2 + 6H_2O \rightarrow C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2 \)

Example 3: What is the role of chlorophyll?

Solution: It absorbs light energy to drive photosynthesis.

Example 4: Why do plants kept in the dark not produce starch?

Solution: No light → no photosynthesis → no glucose → no starch storage.

Example 5: A plant is tested with iodine after being exposed to sunlight. Why does the leaf turn blue-black?

Solution: Because starch has been produced, showing photosynthesis occurred.

Example 6: Why are stomata important in photosynthesis?

Solution: They allow carbon dioxide in and oxygen out.

Example 7: Explain how photosynthesis links to respiration.

Solution: Photosynthesis produces glucose and oxygen, which are reactants for respiration.

Example 8: Why do farmers use greenhouses with extra CO₂ supply?

Solution: Higher CO₂ concentration increases the rate of photosynthesis and crop yield.
